Seeing the World with my Son
<p>Galapagos 2015</p>

Galapagos Islands 2015

As a pastor and a missionary, I have had the privilege of visiting a number of places throughout the world. Over the last 10 years, I have been to South America several times, working in Colombia and Ecuador extensively and, to a lesser extent, in the jungle regions of Brazil and Peru.

I have always wanted to be able to bring my children with me. In March of 2015 I finally had the opportunity to take my oldest son with me on a missionary trip to the Galapagos Islands. That journey offered several firsts for him: his first airline flight, his first visit to another continent, his first immersion into a culture where he was the outsider and barely spoke the language at all.

Here is a picture of Jay as we approach the airport in Quito, Ecuador:


We had to wait a day to meet some friends and arrange our flight, so we spent a little time sightseeing in Quito before catching our flight to the islands. The city has an old European feel to it, and is very picturesque:


Jay took to foreign travel like an old pro. Soon enough, we hit the islands, and although we were going to spend most of our time indoors talking to people, I also tried to make sure that he got a chance to see some of the sights. Santa Cruz Island is the most heavily populated in the Galapagos. Its main city, Puerto Ayora, is geared toward eco-tourists. It is the home of Charles Darwin Research Station and contains around 20,000 people. The Pacific Ocean is never very far away.


Jay had the opportunity to sample some of the local food and experience the local method of doing laundry by hand!



He made a lot of new friends:



At the time, he missed fast food and strong internet connections and could not wait to get back home to his normal life. But since then, he has talked about it as a high point in his life. He misses the people that he met there and the experiences that he had. And he has a much different perspective on life than he used to. I am looking forward to traveling with him again, and to allowing my other kids to encounter other cultures as well.
